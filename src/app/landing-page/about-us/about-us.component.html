Company

Applying for freelance has several advantages for a company. The first is flexibility: a freelancer will often be available over relatively long time slots, as long as he works for a defined mission and precise, with a duration set in advance (or several missions in the case of a client recurrent). In fact, an independent worker will be totally dedicated to the mission that is asked of him. This brings us to the second advantage, namely speed. Since the work is one shot, the delivery of the requested work is fast. Applying to freelance also allows you to benefit from expertise and skills in a specific area, and thus ensure quality work. Indeed, because of the strong competitiveness between freelance, where we must constantly stand out, freelancers must be professional in order to keep a good contact with their client and therefore make a point of honor to satisfy. Last but not least, a freelancer from a point of view of someone outside the company can bring you a different perspective on your project. This can allow you to take a step back from your work and bring a new look that is not negligible to boost creativity and innovation in your business.

Freelancer

A freelancer is a self-employed person who can be remunerated for one or more missions. A freelancer is his own boss: with him, no intermediary, we work live. Unlike an employee, he does not sign a work contract with the company (or a quotation), and the collaboration between the two parties lasts only the time of a project, generally, several days or even weeks at more. Remuneration issue, the payment is done through a system of invoices, which the freelance addresses to the customer. He can then choose to charge at the hourly rate, the daily rate, or even following a package per mission, depending on what best suits the nature of the project.
